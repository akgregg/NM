Nichola Manning, BA, MPA

President, NM CONSULTING INC.

Prior to establishing NM CONSULTING INC., Nichola worked in the BC provincial government for 20 years, where she led large-scale organizations and associated programs.



Nichola spent 16 years with the Ministry of Health (MOH). As a senior executive, she chaired many physician, health authority and government committees, such as: the General Practice Services Committee (GPSC), the Health Authority VPs Medicine/MOH Committee (PSSAC), amongst others. As well, Nichola oversaw the development and implementation of many health programs and initiatives, such as: Physician Master Agreement, Divisions of Family Practice, ‘A GP for Me’ Patient Attachment Initiative, the Nurse Practitioners for BC program, the Patients as Partners Initiative, the Lifetime Prevention Schedule, amongst many others.



As Assistant Deputy Minister (ADM) of the Medical Services Health Human Resources Division in MOH, Nichola had oversight of the Medical Services Plan. She led primary health care reform, and physician negotiations in addition to maintaining and establishing strategic relationships among government, physicians, health authorities, and physician organizations.



In September 2013, she was appointed ADM of the Employment and Labour Market Services Division at the Ministry of Social Development and Social Innovation. Here she provided leadership to the nationally recognized WorkBC employment program, assisting unemployed British Columbians return to the workforce. And as a result, has developed a comprehensive understanding of employment programming as she formerly oversaw $300 million in WorkBC Employment Service Center contracts with not-for-profit and private employment agencies and was a member of the BC Labour Market Priorities Board that had responsibility to implement the BC Jobs Plan and Skills for Jobs Blueprint.
